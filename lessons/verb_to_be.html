The Verb "to be" in Hungarian (A létige: van)

This lesson covers the Hungarian verb "to be" (lenni), its present tense forms, and how it is used in everyday Hungarian. Understanding this verb is essential for talking about identity, professions, nationality, age, and more.

Present Tense Forms

The verb "to be" in Hungarian is lenni (infinitive). In the present tense, it is usually expressed as follows:

Pronoun (English) Pronoun (Hungarian) Form Example
I aménvagyokÉn vagyok tanár. (I am a teacher.)
you are (singular)tevagyTe vagy diák. (You are a student.)
he/she/it isővanŐ mérnök. (He/She is an engineer.)
we aremivagyunkMi vagyunk magyarok. (We are Hungarians.)
you are (plural)tivagytokTi vagytok barátok. (You are friends.)
they areőkvannakŐk diákok. (They are students.)

Usage Notes: When is "van" omitted?

  • In the present tense, van and vannak are omitted when the predicate is a noun or an adjective.
    Example: "Ő tanár." (He/She is a teacher.)
    "A ház nagy." (The house is big.)
  • "Van" is used when expressing existence, location, or when the predicate is not a noun/adjective.
    Example: "A könyv az asztalon van." (The book is on the table.)

More Examples

  • Én vagyok Dániel. (I am Dániel.)
  • Te vagy tanár? (Are you a teacher?)
  • Ő magyar. (He/She is Hungarian.)
  • Mi diákok vagyunk. (We are students.)
  • Ti budapestiek vagytok. (You are from Budapest.)
  • Ők huszonévesek. (They are in their twenties.)
